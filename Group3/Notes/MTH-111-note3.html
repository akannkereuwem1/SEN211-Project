PARTIAL FRACTION

Partial fractions are a way of expressing a rational function as a sum of simpler fractions. This technique is useful for integrating rational functions,solving differential equations, and finding inverse Laplace transforms. Types of Partial Fractions: - _Simple Partial Fractions_: of the form A/(x-a), where A is a constant and a is a real number - _Repeated Partial Fractions_: of the form A/(x-a)^n, where n is a positive integer - _Partial Fractions with Quadratic Factors_: of the form (Ax+B)/(x^2+px+q), where A, B, p, and q are constants Method of Partial Fractions: 1. Factor the denominator of the rational function into linear and quadratic factors. 2. Write the partial fraction decomposition with unknown constants. 3. Multiply both sides of the equation by the denominator to eliminate fractions. 4. Expand the right-hand side and equate coefficients of like terms. 5. Solve the resulting system of equations for the unknown constants. Examples: - Express the rational function 1/(x^2+2x+1) as partial fractions. - Integrate the rational function 2/(x^2-4) using partial fractions. Tips for Working with Partial Fractions: - Factor the denominator carefully to ensure that all factors are accounted for. - Use the correct form of the partial fraction decomposition based on the type of factor. - Check your work by plugging the partial fraction decomposition back into the original equation. Common Mistakes to Avoid: - Forgetting to factor the denominator completely. - Using the wrong form of the partial fraction decomposition. - Failing to check the work by plugging the partial fraction decomposition back into the original equation.
