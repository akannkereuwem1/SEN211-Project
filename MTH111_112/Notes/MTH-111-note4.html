COMPLEX NUMBERS

Complex numbers are numbers that can be expressed in the form a + bi, where a and b are real numbers and i is the imaginary unit, satisfying i^2 = -1. Definition and Notation: Complex Number: a number of the form a + bi, where a and b are real numbers Real Part: the real number a in the complex number a + bi Imaginary Part: the real number b in the complex number a + bi Modulus: the distance of the complex number from the origin, denoted as |a + bi| Argument: the angle between the positive real axis and the line segment connecting the origin to the complex number Operations on Complex Numbers: Addition: (a + bi) + (c + di) = (a + c) + (b + d)i Subtraction: (a + bi) - (c + di) = (a - c) + (b - d)i Multiplication: (a + bi)(c + di) = (ac - bd) + (ad + bc)i Division: (a + bi)/(c + di) = ((ac + bd)/(c^2 + d^2)) + ((bc - ad)/(c^2 + d^2))i Conjugate: the complex number a - bi is the conjugate of a + bi Modulus and Argument: the modulus and argument of a complex number can be used to represent it in polar form Applications of Complex Numbers: Algebra and Geometry: complex numbers are used to solve algebraic equations and represent geometric transformations Calculus and Analysis: complex numbers are used to extend the concepts of calculus and analysis to complex-valued functions Physics and Engineering: complex numbers are used to represent and analyze AC circuits, signals, and systems Examples: Simplify the expression: (2 + 3i)(4 - 5i) Find the modulus and argument of the complex number: 3 + 4i Use complex numbers to solve the equation: x^2 + 4 = 0 Tips for Working with Complex Numbers: Understand the definition and notation of complex numbers. Practice performing operations on complex numbers, such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. Use the properties of complex numbers, such as conjugates and modulus, to simplify expressions and solve equations.
