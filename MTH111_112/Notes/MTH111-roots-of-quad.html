ROOTS OF QUADRATIC

A quadratic equation is a polynomial equation of degree two, which can be written in the form ax^2 + bx + c = 0, where a, b, and c are constants.
The roots of a quadratic equation are the values of x that satisfy the equation. Methods for Finding Roots: Factoring Method: factor the quadratic expression into two binomials, and then set each binomial equal to zero to find the roots. Quadratic Formula Method: use the quadratic formula x = (-b ± √(b^2 - 4ac)) / 2a to find the roots. Graphical Method: graph the quadratic function and find the x-intercepts, which represent the roots. Types of Roots: Real and Distinct Roots: two real and distinct values of x that satisfy the equation. Real and Repeated Roots: two real and equal values of x that satisfy the equation. Complex Roots: two complex values of x that satisfy the equation. Quadratic Formula: x = (-b ± √(b^2 - 4ac)) / 2a: the quadratic formula used to find the roots of a quadratic equation. Discriminant (b^2 - 4ac): the expression under the square root in the quadratic formula, which determines the nature of the roots. Examples: Find the roots of the quadratic equation x^2 + 5x + 6 = 0 using the factoring method. Use the quadratic formula to find the roots of the quadratic equation x^2 - 3x - 4 = 0. Tips for Working with Quadratic Equations: Check if the quadratic expression can be factored easily. Use the quadratic formula when factoring is not possible. Always check the discriminant to determine the nature of the roots.
