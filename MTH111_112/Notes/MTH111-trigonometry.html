MATHEMATICAL INDUCTION

Trigonometry is the study of triangles, particularly triangles with right angles (90 degrees).
It involves the relationships between the sides and angles of triangles. Basic Concepts: Angles: measured in degrees, minutes, and seconds Triangles: classified as acute, right, obtuse, or straight Trigonometric Ratios: relationships between the sides of a right triangle Trigonometric Ratios: Sine (sin) : opposite side / hypotenuse Cosine (cos) : adjacent side / hypotenuse Tangent (tan) : opposite side / adjacent side Cosecant (cosec) : hypotenuse / opposite side Secant (sec) : hypotenuse / adjacent side Cotangent (cot) : adjacent side / opposite side Trigonometric Identities: Pythagorean Identity : sin^2(x) + cos^2(x) = 1 Sum and Difference Identities : sin(a+b) = sin(a)cos(b) + cos(a)sin(b), etc. Double Angle Identities : sin(2x) = 2sin(x)cos(x), etc. Graphs of Trigonometric Functions: Sine and Cosine Graphs : periodic graphs with amplitude and period Tangent Graph : periodic graph with vertical asymptotes Applications of Trigonometry: Navigation and Surveying : calculating distances and angles in navigation and surveying Physics and Engineering : modeling periodic phenomena, such as sound waves and light waves Computer Graphics : creating 3D models and animations Examples: Evaluate the expression: sin(30°) + cos(60°) Prove the identity: sin(a+b) = sin(a)cos(b) + cos(a)sin(b) Find the equation of the graph: y = 2sin(x) + 1 Tips for Working with Trigonometry: Understand the definitions of trigonometric ratios and identities. Practice solving problems involving trigonometric functions and identities. Visualize and graph trigonometric functions to understand their behavior.
